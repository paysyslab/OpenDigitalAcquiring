
# Acquiring Suite — Get Started

The **Acquiring Suite** is a unified merchant acquiring portfolio designed to support RAAST-based P2M payments, card-based POS acquiring, and a hybrid RAAST + POS model within a single, extensible platform. It is built for banks and financial institutions that want to offer modern merchant payment acceptance while maintaining strong controls around compliance, operations, and scalability.

This portfolio enables institutions to onboard merchants once and serve them across multiple payment rails and acceptance channels, without duplicating systems or operational processes.




## What the Acquiring Suite Solves

Banks and acquirers typically face fragmented acquiring landscapes:

- Separate platforms for card POS and instant payment rails.
- Inconsistent merchant onboarding and KYC processes.
- Limited branch-level visibility and operational control.
- Higher operational cost due to duplicated settlement and reporting flows.

The Acquiring Suite addresses these challenges by providing:

- A single acquiring backbone for multiple payment rails.
- Unified merchant lifecycle management across all acceptance types.
- Configurable routing and settlement logic aligned with local regulations.
- Modular deployment, allowing institutions to start small and scale.

## Portfolio Coverage

The Acquiring Suite is structured into three closely integrated acquiring models.

### 1. RAAST P2M Acquiring

RAAST Acquiring enables merchants to accept Person-to-Merchant (P2M) payments through Pakistan’s instant payment system. Customers initiate payments from mobile banking or wallet applications, and transactions are completed in real time with immediate confirmation.

This model primarily uses QR-based acceptance, removing the dependency on card networks or physical POS hardware. Merchants can be enabled with static or dynamic QR codes, making it a low-cost and highly scalable acquiring option.

RAAST Acquiring is well suited for small and medium merchants, high-frequency digital payments, and use cases where instant settlement visibility is essential. The platform manages merchant enablement, QR lifecycle, transaction monitoring, and settlement in alignment with RAAST P2M regulatory requirements.


### 2. POS Acquiring (Card-Based)

POS Acquiring supports card-present transactions across physical and software-based acceptance channels, including POS terminals, MPOS devices, and SoftPOS applications.

This model allows banks to offer traditional card acceptance while retaining full control over merchant onboarding, terminal lifecycle, transaction processing, and settlement operations. The platform is device-agnostic and designed to meet scheme and bank-level security and compliance standards.

Paysys is certified for acquiring with **Visa**, **Mastercard**, **UnionPay**, and **PayPak**, enabling banks to launch and scale POS acquiring services with confidence.

POS Acquiring is ideal for established retail merchants, higher-value transactions, and merchants requiring card acceptance for both domestic and international customers, with full visibility into authorizations, reversals, refunds, and settlement reporting.

### 3. RAAST + POS (Hybrid Acquiring)

Hybrid Acquiring combines **RAAST P2M** and **POS card acquiring** into a single merchant setup. Merchants can accept instant account-based payments and traditional card payments without being onboarded or managed through separate systems.

This approach enables banks to offer a flexible and future-ready acquiring proposition where merchants can support multiple payment methods under one profile. Operations teams benefit from centralized monitoring, unified reporting, and reduced operational overhead, while merchants gain the freedom to serve a wider customer base.

Hybrid Acquiring is especially effective for merchants transitioning from cash or cards to instant payments, or those that require both payment rails to support diverse customer preferences.
